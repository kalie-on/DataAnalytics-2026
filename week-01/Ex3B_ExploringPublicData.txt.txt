1. Start by reviewing the data.gov home page. What is this?

Data.gov is the U.S. government’s open data website. It gives everyone free access to data from government agencies that can be used for research, projects, or decision-making. 

2. Go to Most Viewed Datasets. How many datasets are listed?

There are about 402,374 datasets listed.

3. Find the Topic Category for Human Health. Review the range of data sources
available for this topic. Why do you think these were tagged with the Human Health category?

These datasets talk about things that affect people’s health—like food access, nutrition, air quality, and hospital care. They all connect to human health and well-beings. 

4. Go to the Food Price Outlook. Review the catalog page for this dataset. How can you navigate to additional datasets from the USDA?

On the Food Price Outlook page, click the organization name “Department of Agriculture (USDA)” to go to their main page and see more USDA datasets.

5. Go back to the data.gov home page. This time, click on Datasets by Organization.

From the Data.gov home page, click “Datasets by Organization” to see data grouped by government agencies.

6. Try searching for your city, county, and state. What organizations are returned?
.
When I searched for New Castle, New Castle County, and Delaware, I saw results from the U.S. Geological Survey, Census Bureau, NOAA, and the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).

7. Search for USDA, and click into the result. Within the USDA datasets, search for food composition. Notice that the top three results are all datasets concerning nutrition information. Try searching the USDA page for nutrition. How does that affect your
results?

When I searched food composition under USDA, I found nutrition-related data. Searching nutrition gave more results, like Food Data Central and the Food and Nutrient Database. Changing search terms gives different and sometimes broader results.

8. Go back to the results for food composition, and click on FoodData Central. Review the catalog page for this data source, then click on the button for Visit Page. Take some time to explore the FoodData Central website.

a) Try searching for butter, or any of your favorite foods. What kind of results do you get? What happens when you click on a result?

When I searched for butter, I got results like salted, unsalted, or peanut butter. Clicking a result showed details such as nutrients and serving sizes.
